Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

Effective Date: 2025-12-05

Parties
- Discloser: Casey Thompson (the “Discloser”)
- Recipient: Sam Rodriguez (the “Recipient”)
- Externals engaged by Recipient: Vendor A (Cloud Security Provider), Vendor B (Compliance Auditor)

Definition of Confidential Information
- Includes all non-public information disclosed in connection with the Casey-fast initiative, including data, documents, code, threat models mapped to Epics M0–M4, data-sensitivity tagging, NDA/least-privilege notes, data-access logs, as well as business plans, financials, and vendor details.

Obligations
- Each Party shall hold Confidential Information in confidence, use it solely to evaluate/participate in the Casey-fast initiative, and disclose only to personnel on a need-to-know basis under NDA.
- Implement reasonable safeguards, restrict copies, and mark or identify Confidential Information as confidential if disclosed orally.
- Maintain auditable data-access logs for Confidential Information, including user identity, timestamp, purpose, and scope; provide logs within 5 business days of request or upon termination.

Exclusions
- Information that is public, already known, independently developed, or rightfully obtained from a third party without breach.

Term
- This Agreement lasts four (4) years from the Effective Date.

Return/Destruction
- Upon written request, return or destroy Confidential Information and certify destruction; backups may be retained as required for legal/compliance purposes but must remain confidential and restricted.

Third-Party/Subcontractors
- Externals and any subcontractors must be bound by written NDA terms at least as protective as this Agreement. Recipient will ensure access to Confidential Information is strictly on a need-to-know basis and that logs are maintained for auditable review.

No License
- No license or right to any Confidential Information is granted by this Agreement, except the limited right to use for the Casey-fast initiative as stated.

Governing Law and Jurisdiction
- This Agreement is governed by the laws of Delaware. Any disputes shall be resolved in the state or federal courts located in Wilmington, Delaware. The Externals Annex is incorporated by reference and governs external parties.

Remedies
- Breach may result in injunctive relief and any other remedies available at law or in equity.
